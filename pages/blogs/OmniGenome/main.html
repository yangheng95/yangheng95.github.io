OmniGenome: Aligning RNA Sequences with Secondary Structures in Genomic Foundation Models

Published on: October 6, 2024

Authors: Heng Yang and Ke Li, University of Exeter

Introduction

RNA sequences and their secondary structures play crucial roles in biological processes, yet existing genomic foundation models (FMs) have failed to properly align sequences with structures. OmniGenome aims to address this issue by enabling bidirectional mappings between RNA sequences and secondary structures. The model introduces a flexible paradigm to facilitate RNA design and secondary structure prediction with improved performance across diverse tasks.

Sequence-Structure Alignment in Foundation Models

The main challenge addressed by OmniGenome is the lack of alignment between RNA nucleotide sequences and their folded structures in current models. This alignment is essential because it allows genomic information to flow freely between sequences and structures. Existing models, like RNA-FM and RNA-MSM, fail to solve most RNA design tasks because they overlook this critical alignment. OmniGenome introduces sequence-structure alignment to resolve this limitation.

Str2Seq and Seq2Str Mappings

OmniGenome supports two main mapping tasks:

  • Str2Seq (Structure to Sequence Mapping): OmniGenome predicts RNA sequences from given structures, significantly improving performance on tasks like RNA design.
  • Seq2Str (Sequence to Structure Mapping): It also predicts the secondary structure of RNA sequences with greater accuracy than traditional models.

This bidirectional mapping capability enhances OmniGenome's utility for a wide range of genome modelling tasks.

Methodology

OmniGenome uses a Transformer-based architecture with bidirectional multi-head attention to achieve sequence-structure alignment. Two variants of the model were developed: OmniGenome52M (52 million parameters) and OmniGenome186M (186 million parameters).

Pre-training Objectives

The pre-training of OmniGenome is designed around three main objectives:

  • Str2Seq Loss (LStr2Seq): Trains the model to predict sequence bases from structure-contextualized inputs.
  • Seq2Str Loss (LSeq2Str): Supervises the prediction of secondary structures from sequences.

Results and Evaluation

OmniGenome was evaluated on several benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance across multiple tasks, such as RNA design and zero-shot secondary structure prediction:

  • RNA Design: OmniGenome solved 74% of puzzles on the EternaV2 benchmark, compared to just 3% solved by RNA-FM.
  • Zero-shot Secondary Structure Prediction: Achieved a macro-F1 score of 74.85%, outperforming fine-tuned models and bioinformatics methods like ViennaRNA.

Conclusion

OmniGenome sets a new standard for genomic foundation models by addressing the challenge of sequence-structure alignment. It outperforms state-of-the-art models in both RNA and DNA modelling tasks, making it a powerful tool for researchers working in genomics.
